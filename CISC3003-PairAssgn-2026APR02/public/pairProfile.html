Our Values and Beliefs

Core Principles Behind Our Work

Clarity Over Complexity

A strong website should enable visitors to understand its key messages quickly, rather than distracting them with unnecessary decoration.

Responsive Design Matters

A modern website should provide a consistent and natural browsing experience across mobile, tablet, and desktop devices.

Content and Design Work Together

Design should not be detached from content; instead, layout should support communication rather than obscure the message itself.

Collaboration Supports Quality

As Computer Science students, we value improving details through collaboration and enhancing our aesthetic judgment, development skills, and problem-solving ability through practice.

Our Blog Posts

Ideas That Shape Our Design Practice

Blog Post 1

Why Responsive Design Matters in Modern Websites

As more users access websites through mobile phones and tablets, responsive design is no longer an optional feature but a foundational requirement of modern web design. Through studying responsive design, we have developed a stronger understanding of the practical significance of user experience in web development.

Blog Post 2

What We Learned from Building Our First Multi-Page Website

When constructing our first complete multi-page website, our most significant realization was not simply that the technical work was demanding, but that consistency of style, structural clarity, and smooth navigation across multiple pages were equally important.

Blog Post 3

Why Good Navigation Is Essential in Web Design

A clear navigation structure enables visitors to locate the information they need efficiently and also enhances the professionalism of the entire website. In our design practice, navigation has consistently been treated as one of the most essential elements.

Blog Post 4

How We Understand Web Design as Computer Science Students

We believe that web design should not be understood merely as the visual decoration of a page, but rather as the integration of computer technology, information organization, and visual communication. We prefer to approach web design as a form of user-oriented problem solving.
